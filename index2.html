Adventure Time

Adventure Life is an adventure travel company offering private journeys, small group tours, and expedition voyages throughout the world. From their beginnings as a Latin America specialist in South America and Central America land tours, they have grown to now offer trips around the globe including less conventional destinations such as Africa, the Arctic, Antarctica and Alaska. Their focus is nature, cultural, and active travel, and they apply ecotourism[1] principles to their tour and cruise programs. Most trips have a maximum group size of 12, yet typically run with 6 or fewer travelers. A large portion of travelers arrange customized or private itineraries. Since their founding in 1999, over 24,000 clients have traveled with the company.

Adventure Life was founded by Brian Morgan in 1999.[2] Morgan was working as a consultant in Quito for CARE in the late 1990s, but decided to return home to Montana. While applying for jobs in Montana, Morgan planned a group tour back to Ecuador and promoted it locally. This experience made him recognize that guided tours were a service in demand, and a niche industry that his own travel experiences and Ecuadorian connections had prepared him to explore further.[3] Adventure Life launched in February 1999 with Spanish language study-trips, internships, and tours to Peru and Ecuador. Office was initially located in Havre, Montana, and Morgan hired the first staff member. During the first year of business, Adventure Life had fewer than 100 travelers.

BIKING

Biking is a popular and fulfilling way of life that offers a multitude of benefits to individuals and communities. From a personal perspective, biking promotes physical fitness and enhances cardiovascular health. It provides a low-impact workout that is gentle on the joints, reduces the risk of certain health conditions, and helps maintain a healthy weight. Moreover, biking contributes to environmental sustainability as it produces no emissions and alleviates traffic congestion. It also proves to be a cost-effective mode of transportation, saving money on fuel and parking fees. Beyond the physical and environmental advantages, biking allows individuals to immerse themselves in their surroundings, fostering a greater appreciation for nature and their community. It serves as a liberating and independent activity, enabling cyclists to choose their own routes and explore new areas at their own pace. Additionally, biking encourages social interaction, fostering a sense of camaraderie among cyclists and promoting community engagement. Whether for leisure or commuting, biking is a lifelong skill that can be enjoyed by people of all ages and fitness levels, contributing to a culture of well-being and sustainable living.

PARA GLIDING

Paragliding is a thrilling adventure sport where individuals glide through the air using a paraglider, a foot-launched aircraft. It involves flying a lightweight fabric wing, controlled by shifting body weight and brake lines. Launching can be done from hillsides, mountains, or designated sites, and pilots can steer, climb, descend, and perform maneuvers during flight. Safety is paramount, requiring proper training, experienced guides, and adherence to safety protocols. Flight duration depends on wind conditions, skill, and thermal updrafts, ranging from minutes to hours. Paragliding can cover varying distances, with cross-country flights reaching tens or hundreds of kilometers. It encompasses different forms like recreational flying, cross-country flights, acrobatics, and competitions. Acro paragliding involves aerobatic maneuvers, while tandem flights allow passengers to experience paragliding with an experienced pilot. Training and certification are essential, covering ground handling, launching, landing, emergency procedures, and theoretical knowledge. Paragliding offers a unique opportunity to enjoy the freedom of flight and breathtaking aerial views.

SURFING

The surfing glossary of terms is a complete list of expressions used by surfers on the waves, at the beach, and outside in the real world. Some of these words grew in popularity and became widely used daily expressions. Surf speak is a vibrant reality and can be genuinely entertaining. Many times, surfer slang represents the good and bad of the so-called surf culture. But the truth is that the surfing terminology keeps evolving and adding new jargon to its dictionary pages.

Surfing is a thrilling water sport that involves riding waves on a surfboard. Surfers paddle out to the ocean, wait for suitable waves, and then catch them using their boards. The key is to balance and maneuver on the wave's face, utilizing the power and momentum of the water. Surfers use various techniques, such as carving, trimming, and performing tricks like cutbacks and aerials. Surfing can be done in different types of waves, ranging from small and gentle to large and powerful. It requires strength, agility, and wave knowledge. Surfing is not only a physical activity but also a way to connect with nature and experience the pure joy of riding the ocean's energy.
